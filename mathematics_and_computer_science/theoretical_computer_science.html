Theoretical Computer Science Notes

  • Alphabet

    An Alphabet $ \Sigma $ is a finite, non-empty Set.
    Elements of this Set are called Letters.

  • Word

    • A Word $ w $ over $ \Sigma $ is a finite (and possibly empty) sequence of Letters from $ \Sigma$
    • The Length of a word: $ |w| $
    • The empty word has length 0 : $ | \lambda | = 0$

    • Concatenation

      The concatenation of two words:
      $ v = a_{1} ... a_{n}$
      $ w= b_{1} ... b_{m}$
      $ vw = a_{1} ... a_{n}b_{1} ... b_{m}$
    • u is a prefix of w $ \Leftrightarrow \exists s \in \Sigma * : us = w$
    • u is a infix of w $ \Leftrightarrow \exists r,s \in \Sigma * : rus = w$
    • u is a suffix of w $ \Leftrightarrow \exists r \in \Sigma * : ru = w$

    • Iteration

      Let w be a word.
      $ w^{0}:=\lambda, w^{n+1}:= ww^{n} $

    • Reversing

      Let w be a word.
      Case $w=\lambda$ : $w^{R} = \lambda $
      Case $w = va $ with $ v \in \Sigma^{*} , a \in \Sigma : (va)^{R} := a(v^{R}) $

  • Kleene - Star (*)

    $ \Sigma^{*} := \bigcup_{i \in N} \Sigma^{i} $
    $ \Sigma^{+} := \bigcup_{i \geq 1} \Sigma^{i} $

  • Language

    a Set L is a Language over an Alphabet $\Sigma$ , if $ L \subseteq \Sigma^{*}$

    • Concatenation of Languages

      let $L_{1}, L_{2} $ be Languages.
      $ L_{1}L_{2} := \{uv | u \in L_{1}, v \in L_{2}\} $

    • Iterations of a Language

      let $L $ be a Language

      $ L^{0} := \{ \lambda \} , L^{n+1} = LL^{n} $
    • $||L|| $ is the Cardinality of the Language, meaning the number of Words.
    • The set of all languages forms a semigroup with concatenation.
      Concatenation of Languages is associative.
    • Complement
      $ \overline{A} = \Sigma^{*} - A$

  • Grammatik

    A Grammar is a Quadrupel $ G = (\Sigma, N, S, P) $ , where
    $\Sigma$ is an Alphabet,
    N is a finite Set of Non-Terminals with $\Sigma \cap N = \emptyset$
    $ S \in N$ a Start Symbol and
    $ P \subseteq (N \cup \Sigma)^{+} \times (N \cup \Sigma)^{*} $ the finite Set of Production Rules

  • Pushdown Automaton (Kellerautomat)

    (nondeterministic PDA)
    $ M= ( \Sigma, \Gamma, Z, \delta, z_{0}, \# ) $ where

    • $\Sigma $ the Alphabet
    • $\Gamma $ the Keller Alphabet (Pushdown Alphabet)
    • $Z $ a finite Set of States
    • $\delta : Z \times (\Sigma \cup \{\lambda\}) \times \Gamma \rightarrow P_{e}(Z \times \Gamma^{*})$
      • $P_{e}(X) $ meint die Menge der endlichen Teilmengen von X
    • $z_{0} $ the starting state
    • $\# \in \Gamma$ the Bottom symbol of the PDA

    How the PDA should work:

    • Accepting a word happens through an emtpy stack (empty keller)
    • There only needs to be one starting state.
